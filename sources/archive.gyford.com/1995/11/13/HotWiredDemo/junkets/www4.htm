The Web Revolution

The Web Revolution is the fourth in a series of International World Wide Web Conferences. It will bring together researchers, developers, and users working with the World Wide Web.

The conference takes place 11-14 December 1995, in the Boston Marriott Copley Place, and at the Hynes Convention Center.

Participation

In order to attend, you need to register. The deadline for early bird registration, 29 October 1995, has passed.

  • Registration

Most opportunities to formally present work relating to the World Wide Web have already been filled. Papers have been called for, evaluated, and accepted into the conference program. Panelists have been solicited and panel discussions have been developed. Workshops and tutorials have been created. But there is still space available to attend the conference, and to participate in workshops, tutorials, and Developer's Day.

Due to the season and other events in Boston, hotels are filling quickly, so we recommend making reservations as early as possible. Information is available on local hotels, airline discounts, and getting around in Boston.
