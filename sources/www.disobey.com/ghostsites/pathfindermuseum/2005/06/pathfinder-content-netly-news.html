Pathfinder Content: The Netly News


While the great bulk of Pathfinder's staff spent its days processing pre-existing content from its many Content Partners (a process disparaged by some "shovelware"), original content at Pathfinder was, at least for a time, judged to be crucial to the goal of building "the World's Best Web Site" and the Netly News was Pathfinder's highest-profile experiment.



Everything about the Netly News was uncompromisingly original, including the use of a large cow as its emblem, its use of unconventional, unprecictably-shifting page margins, peculiar retro-graphics, and an opinionated, abrasive editorial voice that melded well with the frontier ideology of the early World Wide Web.

About the Museum

Welcome to the Pathfinder Museum. This museum originally went on line in early 2000, but after service interruptions, its resources have long lay dormant on various CD-ROMs and Zip drives. Now, however, because historians have expressed renewed interest in the rise and fall of "The World's Greatest Web Site," the Museum is back on line. We are also lucky to have been bequeathed a scrap book of many images from Pathfinder.com's heyday that was saved from the dumpster during the site's last days.

The Museum has three different kinds of exhibits: Pathfinder Documents (preserved documents from the site's heyday), Pathfinder Images (snapshots and other images that record the activities of its staff), and Pathfinder Memories (written accounts of life and work at The World's Greatest Web site). I plan on updating the Museum from time to time with new exhibits; if you have any questions about the Museums or would like to contribute material, please send e-mail.

Thanks,
Steve Baldwin
Curator, The Pathfinder Museum.
